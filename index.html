Data Collection Platform

mWater Platform

The data was collected using mWater, a comprehensive water monitoring platform that enables:

  • Real-time data collection through mobile devices
  • GPS coordinate capture for precise location mapping
  • Photo documentation of water point conditions
  • Standardized survey protocols ensuring data quality
  • Cloud-based data storage and management

Data Quality

Comprehensive 8-step quality improvement process implemented

Data Quality Enhancements

Standardization

Functional status coded to standard categories, boolean fields standardized, problem categories grouped logically

Validation

GPS coordinates validated, household counts validated, cross-field logical consistency checks implemented

Enhancement

Multi-value fields expanded, missing data standardized, comprehensive audit trail maintained

Key Variables

Core Variables

  • water_point_id: Unique identifier for each water point
  • functional_status: Current operational status (FUNC/PARTIAL/NOT_FUNC/ABANDONED)
  • latitude/longitude: GPS coordinates for mapping
  • households_count: Number of households served

Service & Quality Variables

  • current_problem: Categorized problems (EQUIPMENT_FAILURE, LOW_FLOW, etc.)
  • service_provider_type: Type of maintenance/operation provider
  • quality_issues_type: Water quality concerns (ODOR, SALINITY, etc.)
  • submitted_date: Survey completion date

Key Insights & Recommendations

Service Provider Impact

Water points with active service providers show significantly higher functionality rates, emphasizing the importance of maintenance systems.

Common Problems

Equipment failure is the most common issue, followed by low flow problems, indicating need for preventive maintenance programs.

Quality Concerns

Water quality issues, particularly salinity and turbidity, affect functionality and require targeted interventions.

Community Access

Household utilization patterns show varying access levels, with opportunities for optimization and expansion.
